Shounen

Shounen anime targets young male viewers and typically features action-packed storylines, adventurous journeys, and themes of friendship and personal growth. Famous examples include Naruto, Bleach, Dragon Ball Z, One Piece, and My Hero Academia

Seinen

Seinen anime caters to adult male audiences with mature themes and intricate storytelling. Notable series include "Attack on Titan" depicting humanity's struggle against giants, "Berserk" following a lone warrior's quest for revenge in a dark medieval world, and "Vinland Saga" which explores Viking history and the protagonist's journey for redemption

Isekai

Isekai anime transports protagonists from their ordinary world to fantastical realms or parallel universes. Notable series include "Sword Art Online" where players are trapped in a virtual reality MMORPG, "Re:Zero - Starting Life in Another World" featuring a boy who discovers he can return to a checkpoint after dying in a fantasy world, and "No Game No Life" following siblings who excel in gaming and are summoned to a world ruled by games

Sci-Fi

Sci-fi anime ventures into futuristic realms, technology, and speculative ideas. Highlighted series encompass "Code Geass" featuring political intrigue and a rebel leader's quest for revolution and "Ghost in the Shell" exploring cybernetic enhancements and philosophical questions about identity. "Steins;Gate" follows time travel experiments and the ramifications of changing history.
